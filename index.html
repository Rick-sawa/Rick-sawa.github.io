Experience & Research

Master's Research: Inverse Problems in Medical CT

I am studying Photon Counting CT, a next-generation medical technology. A major problem with this technology is "Pulse Pile-up," where multiple light particles are counted as one. I solve this using an inverse problem approach. I model the physical movement of electrons in the semiconductor and use statistical methods (like Bayesian inference) to estimate the correct signals from the data.

Internship at BETA CAE Systems

I worked as a software developer intern for six months at a simulation software company in Greece. Using my statistics knowledge, I proposed and implemented a new metric to evaluate the uncertainty in Monte Carlo simulations. Working in an environment where English was the main language helped me improve my practical coding skills and communication ability.

Bachelor's Research: Strain Measurement Method

I developed a method to measure very small deformations (strain) on the surface of objects without touching them. The key idea was combining a special "compliant mechanism" (which physically magnifies the strain) with image processing. Experiments showed that this method effectively improves measurement accuracy.

Projects

Image Recognition Sorting Robot

I developed a robot that sorts objects using machine learning and control engineering. The robot uses a camera to identify if an object is a dog or a cat, then uses an arm to pick it up and automatically drives along a line to the correct place.

Two-Wheel Inverted Pendulum Robot

I designed a self-balancing robot using PID control. It uses a gyro sensor to detect tilt and a motor encoder to check speed. The system calculates the best motor output in real-time to keep the robot standing and moving stably.

Gyroscope Two-Wheel Robot

I built this robot for the Science Koshien National Competition as a representative of Yamanashi Prefecture. Unlike normal bikes, it uses a fast-spinning disk (gyro effect) to keep its balance and drive automatically. The unique design was highly evaluated in the competition.
